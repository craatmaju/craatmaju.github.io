Programme Overview

About the Summer School

The NLP Summer School Karachi 2026 is a five-day intensive programme organised by the NLP Community of Karachi. The programme covers the full spectrum of modern NLP — from foundational text processing techniques to state-of-the-art Transformer models, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), and Large Language Models (LLMs).

The programme combines lectures, demonstrations, hands-on coding labs, and a capstone mini-project to provide both theoretical understanding and practical experience using Python, Google Colab, Hugging Face, and LangChain.

Special emphasis is placed on NLP challenges and opportunities for Pakistani languages, including Urdu, Roman Urdu, Sindhi, Punjabi, Pashto, and code-mixed social media text.

Why Attend?

What You Gain

  • A structured introduction to modern NLP and LLM technologies
  • Hands-on experience using Python, Google Colab, Hugging Face, and LangChain
  • Practical exposure to text classification, transformers, BERT, and RAG systems
  • Understanding of NLP challenges in Pakistani and low-resource languages
  • Guidance on research directions and industry applications in NLP and Generative AI

Who Should Attend?

  • Final-year undergraduate students
  • MS / MPhil students
  • Early-stage PhD students
  • Young faculty members
  • Software developers interested in NLP
  • AI/ML researchers beginning work in NLP

Prerequisites

  • Basic Python programming knowledge
  • Basic understanding of machine learning
  • Familiarity with Google Colab
  • Basic linear algebra (helpful but not mandatory)

Five-Day Programme

Schedule

Day 1 · July 20
Foundations of NLP and Text Processing
▾
Topics
  • Introduction to NLP applications and language challenges
  • Text preprocessing, normalization, and tokenization
  • Regular expressions, stemming, and lemmatization
  • Urdu, Roman Urdu, and Pakistani language preprocessing
Hands-on Lab
  • Build a basic text preprocessing pipeline
  • Compare preprocessing for English, Urdu, and Roman Urdu text
Day 2 · July 21
Classical NLP and Text Classification
▾
Topics
  • Bag-of-Words, TF-IDF, and n-gram representations
  • Feature extraction and sparse vector representations
  • Classical machine learning algorithms for NLP
  • Evaluation metrics and error analysis
Hands-on Lab
  • Build a sentiment classifier using classical machine learning
  • Compare Naive Bayes, Logistic Regression, and SVM models
Day 3 · July 22
Embeddings, Sequence Modeling, and Transformers
▾
Topics
  • Word embeddings and distributed representations
  • RNNs, Attention, and sequence modeling
  • Transformer architecture and applications
Hands-on Lab
  • Compare classical and embedding-based approaches
  • Using LLMs and SLMs in practice
Day 4 · July 23
BERT, Fine-tuning, and Large Language Models
▾
Topics
  • BERT and multilingual transformer models
  • Fine-tuning pre-trained models
  • Prompt engineering principles and techniques
Hands-on Lab
  • Fine-tune BERT for text classification
  • Evaluate transformer-based NLP models and solutions
Day 5 · July 24
RAG, Modern NLP, and Responsible AI
▾
Topics
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
  • Ethics, bias, and responsible NLP
  • NLP opportunities for Pakistani and low-resource languages
Hands-on Lab & Capstone
  • Build a simple RAG-based question answering system
  • Capstone project presentations

After Five Days

Learning Outcomes

Text & Pipeline

Understand the standard NLP pipeline and clean and preprocess textual data effectively.

Representations

Represent text using Bag-of-Words, TF-IDF, and word embeddings, and train classical NLP models.

Transformers

Understand transformer and BERT architectures and fine-tune models for basic NLP tasks.

LLMs & RAG

Understand LLM fundamentals, prompt engineering, and build a basic RAG system.

Research Directions

Identify research opportunities in Pakistani and low-resource languages.
